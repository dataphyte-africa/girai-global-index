# Country page narrative templates (client review)

> **Superseded for live country pages.** Editorial copy now comes from evidence-grounded fact bundles and generated narratives. See **[country-narratives.md](./country-narratives.md)** and the auto-generated **[country-narratives-index.md](./country-narratives-index.md)**. This document is retained as a record of the former score-tier templates.

This document lists the **full editorial copy** used on GIRAI country pages for score-based narratives. Copy is generated from a country’s numeric score: the same template applies to every country in that score band.

Templates use placeholders:

| Placeholder | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **{Country}** | Country name (e.g. Norway, Nigeria) |
| **{Score}** | Numeric score, shown with one decimal (e.g. 83.5) |
| **{Dimension name}** | One of the five GIRAI dimensions (see list below) |
| **{Pillar name}** | One of the three GIRAI pillars (see list below) |

---

## Score tiers

Every narrative below is chosen from one of five tiers based on the relevant score:

| Tier label (shown in some UI) | Score range |
|-------------------------------|-------------|
| Global Leader | 80 and above |
| Advanced | 60 up to (but not including) 80 |
| Developing | 40 up to (but not including) 60 |
| Emerging | 20 up to (but not including) 40 |
| Nascent | Below 20 |

The same tier bands apply to the **overall index score**, each **dimension score**, and each **pillar score**.

---

## Where each narrative appears

| Page section | Narrative type |
|--------------|----------------|
| Hero (global index score) | Index narrative |
| “How {Country} performs across the five dimensions” | Dimension narrative (one per dimension) |
| “What drives this performance?” — callout box at bottom of each pillar card | Pillar narrative |

**Note:** Pillar cards also show fixed headings, short descriptions, and three factual bullet points derived from evidence data. Those bullets are **not** tier templates; they are built per country from documented frameworks, initiatives, and indicator scores. This document covers only the **tier-based prose** above.

## "Contribution to overall score" bar (pillar cards)

Each pillar card in "What drives this performance?" shows a **Contribution to overall score** percentage with a progress bar, sitting between the static card copy and the three factual bullets. This value is **calculated from the country's scores**, not a tier template.

### What it represents

It is each pillar's **weighted share of the country's composite pillar contribution**. The three cards (AI Policy, CSO Engagement, Enabling Conditions) together add up to roughly 100% (subject to rounding).

### How it is calculated

For a given country, every pillar has a score on a 0–100 scale and an official GIRAI weight:

| Pillar | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| AI Policy | 0.6 |
| CSO Engagement | 0.1 |
| Enabling Conditions | 0.3 |

1. For each pillar, compute **weighted contribution** = pillar score × pillar weight.
2. **Add up** the weighted contributions to get a total.
3. For each pillar, **divide** its weighted contribution by that total and multiply by 100.
4. **Round** the result to a whole number for display.

So: `pillar contribution % = (pillar score × pillar weight ÷ sum of weighted contributions) × 100`.

### Worked example

If a country's pillar scores are AI Policy = 60, CSO Engagement = 30, Enabling Conditions = 50:

- AI Policy weighted contribution = 60 × 0.6 = 36
- CSO Engagement weighted contribution = 30 × 0.1 = 3
- Enabling Conditions weighted contribution = 50 × 0.3 = 15
- Total = 36 + 3 + 15 = 54
- AI Policy → 36 ÷ 54 × 100 ≈ **67%**
- CSO Engagement → 3 ÷ 54 × 100 ≈ **6%**
- Enabling Conditions → 15 ÷ 54 × 100 ≈ **28%**

### Edge cases

- If the weighted contributions add up to zero (or less), every card shows **—** instead of a percentage.
- A pillar with no published score does not add to the weighted total and shows **—** on its own card.

## Editorial notes for reviewers

- Tier copy is **generic**: it does not cite specific laws, programmes, or evidence items. Factual detail on the country page comes from scores, ranks, charts, and the three bullet points per pillar (evidence- and indicator-driven).
- A future content workflow may allow **country-specific overrides** via CMS or generated summaries; until then, these templates are the source of truth for tier-based prose.
- Wording uses UK English spellings where applicable (e.g. “recognise”, “operationalisation”).
